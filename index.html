03 — Singularity

Where
Time
Bends

A black hole contains so much mass in such a tiny space that nothing — not even light — can escape. At the event horizon, time itself dilates to infinity.

"Black holes are where God divided by zero."

— Steven Wright

🌀

EVENT HORIZON

The point of no return. Once crossed, no information can escape — not even at the speed of light.

The Schwarzschild radius r_s = 2GM/c² defines the event horizon. For our Sun, this is ~3 km. Cross it and causality itself breaks — your entire future worldline terminates at the singularity.
⏱

TIME DILATION

Near a black hole, time moves slower relative to distant observers. Gravity warps the flow of time itself.

From GR: t' = t√(1 − r_s/r). At r = 1.5 r_s, clocks tick at 57.7% the rate of a distant observer. At r → r_s, time stops entirely — you never appear to cross the horizon to an outside viewer.
🔭

HAWKING RADIATION

Stephen Hawking theorized that black holes slowly evaporate over cosmic timescales by emitting thermal radiation.

T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B). A stellar black hole radiates at ~60 nanokelvin — far cooler than the CMB (2.7 K), so it actually gains mass from the cosmic background. Only black holes smaller than the Moon would currently evaporate.
∞

SINGULARITY

At the center, density approaches infinity and known physics breaks down. A gateway to the unknown.

GR predicts infinite curvature at r = 0, but most physicists believe quantum gravity regularizes this. The Penrose singularity theorem proves singularities are unavoidable given realistic matter collapse — but their interior structure remains entirely unknown.
